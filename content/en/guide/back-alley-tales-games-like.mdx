### Do Not Feed the Monkeys
In this unique simulation game, you join the Primate Observation Club, an elite group that hacks into security cameras to watch strangers. Much like watching the camera feeds in the alleyways, you monitor multiple screens, take notes on character behavior, and search the web for clues. The golden rule is simple: do not interact with the subjects (the monkeys). However, the temptation to intervene, blackmail, or help them is constant, especially when you need money to pay your rent.

### Beholder
Set in a grim totalitarian state, you play as Carl, a state-appointed landlord of an apartment building. Your official duty is to spy on your tenants, install hidden cameras in their rooms, search their belongings, and profile them for the government. You must balance the demands of a ruthless regime against your own humanity and your family's survival. The oppressive atmosphere and heavy emphasis on voyeurism make this an incredibly close match.

### Orwell: Keeping an Eye on You
For players who enjoy the analytical side of investigation, Orwell puts you in charge of a massive state security program. You must investigate the lives of citizens by searching their social media profiles, reading private chats, and accessing medical records to find perpetrators of national security threats. The game forces you to decide what information to report, knowing that your profiling can ruin innocent lives.

### Cyber Manhunt
This title combines hacking mechanics with narrative puzzle-solving. As an assistant at a major tech firm, you use advanced digital tools to dig up dirty secrets, crack passwords, and perform social engineering to solve complex cases. It perfectly captures the modern digital detective fantasy, offering highly immersive stories that deal with cyberbullying, data privacy, and online manipulation.
